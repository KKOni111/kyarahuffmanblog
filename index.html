A Behind the Scenes Look at the Artist Behind the Art Pieces!

Hello everybody! My name is Kyara Kaileena Huffman and I have been drawing since I was a young child! For this blog, I wanted to share some of my artworks as well as the inspiration behind the pieces that I draw. I also wanted to share the supplies I use, techniques, inspiration, how long it took to draw something, as well as other fun facts behind the pieces that I draw. As for art supplies, I typically keep it quite simple. My favorite art program to use is Procreate with my ipad 5th generation and apple pencil. I believe that the apps' capabilities to not only draw but to animate as well as its cheap price of 10 dollars make the app the best one to use for digital artists like myself. As for my desktop drawings, I use kritia, a free software that has often been compared to photoshop.As for the subjects that I like to draw the most, I like to draw people, in particular female human characters. I like to draw fan art of shows, games, and movies that I am a fan of.

Sadeena For this piece, I wanted to challenge myself to use a lineless artstyle. For most of my pieces, I will use heavy lines and shade the art with black to distinguish between the different objects in an art piece. However, for this art piece, I had to rely on lighter colors and less of a color range while also making sure that the piece didn't get muddy. 10/25/24
Atom Eve For this piece, I wanted to make a more cinematic and dramatic piece. Becuase of this, I used a canvas size which matches the ratio of a movie scene in most movie theaters.I wanted to practice foreshortening, or the technique that makes objects seem 3d in a 2d drawing. I wanted the hands to appear as if they were moving forward. 11/04/24
Serai For this piece, I wanted to catpure the swiftness of the character Serai which is from a videogame I played. I used a movie theater ratio size for my campus and referenced dynamic poses. I wanted to surround the character with the powers she uses in order to frame her and show her as the focal point. 11/11/24
